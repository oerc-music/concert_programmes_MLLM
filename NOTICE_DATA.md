# Data Rights and Attribution

## Copyright

**© Bodleian Libraries, University of Oxford.**

The 100 sample concert programme images bundled in [`input/`](input/) (and the
matching binarised versions) are reproduced from historical concert
programmes held by the Bodleian Libraries, University of Oxford, in the
*Gough Adds.* collection. They are provided here **solely to let this demo be
tried out immediately, without requiring users to source their own images**.

The [MIT License](LICENSE) covers only the **code** in this repository. The
images are **not** covered by that license and may be subject to separate
terms of use set by the Bodleian Libraries. Please contact the Bodleian
Libraries directly before any reuse beyond running this demo.

## Source Collections

The sample programmes (1872–1928) document the programming activities of
three Oxford student music societies, catalogued in the Bodleian's SOLO
catalogue as follows (citation form as supplied by the rights holder):

> Bodleian Libraries. 2025. *Oxford University Musical Club and Union
> [Programmes. 1916– ]*: "SOLO" Catalogue Record.
> <https://solo.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/permalink/44OXF_INST/ogbd98/alma990143522580107026>

> Bodleian Libraries. 2025. *Oxford University Musical Club [Programmes &c.,
> 1872–1916]*: "SOLO" Catalogue Record.
> <https://solo.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/permalink/44OXF_INST/ogbd98/alma990143523700107026>

> Bodleian Libraries. 2025. *Oxford University Musical Union [Programmes &c.
> 1884–1916]*: "SOLO" Catalogue Record.
> <https://solo.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/permalink/44OXF_INST/ogbd98/alma990143565930107026>

These correspond to the three layout groups described in the accompanying
DLfM 2026 paper: Oxford University Musical Club (OUMC, 1872–1916), Oxford
University Musical Union (OUMU, 1884–1916), and Oxford University Musical
Club & Union (OUMCU, 1916–1928).

## Important Caveats

- **This is a demonstration sample, not a catalogue.** Annotations produced
  by this tool are AI-generated, unverified first-pass drafts and must not be
  treated as authoritative metadata for these items.
- **Images leave your machine when you run a live annotation.** Google
  Gemini receives the image data you submit, per their terms of service.
  See the README's "Privacy and data handling" section before processing
  other people's cultural heritage material with this tool.
- For the authoritative, peer-reviewed experimental dataset (full-resolution
  source images, ground truth, MLLM outputs, and assessment statistics), see
  the archived snapshot referenced in the paper:
  <https://dx.doi.org/10.5287/ora-n6yg5jgqy>
